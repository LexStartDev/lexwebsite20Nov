FAQ

This FAQ contains information about Form DIR-3 KYC and the applicability and process for filing this form

What is DIR-3 KYC?

The DIR-3 KYC is a new e-form introduced by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA), India in order to conduct an annual KYC of directors of all companies and partners of LLPs.

Where can I find the format for DIR-3 KYC?

Form DIR 3-KYC is available on MCA21 Company Forms Download page at http://www.mca.gov.in/MinistryV2/companyformsdownload.html

Who has to file the form DIR-3 KYC?

Every individual who has been allotted Director Identification Number (DIN) by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) on or before March 31, 2018 and whose DIN is either (i) in “Approved” status, or (ii) inactive due to disqualification of such Director, would be mandatory to file form DIR-3 KYC with the MCA.

I have a DIN but I am not currently serving as director. Do I have to file form DIR-3 KYC?

Yes. Even if you are not currently serving as a director on the board of a company, if you want to retain your DIN, you will have to file form DIR-3 KYC.

What is the last date of filing the form DIR-3 KYC?

The director must file form DIR-3 KYC with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) by August 31, 2018.

What happens if a director is not able to file the form DIR-3 KYC by the deadline?

Failure to file the form DIR-3 KYC by August 31, 2018 will result in the DIN of the director being ‘deactivated’ and consequently disqualifying such director from acting as a director of a company or a partner of a LLP. The DIN can be activated by the director by paying additional filing fees of ₹ 5000.

What are the details required to be provided in the form DIR-3 KYC?

The following information will be required: 1. DIN; 2. Full name; 3. Address; 4. Aadhaar number (if available); 5. Identity proof i.e. PAN (if you are a resident director) or Passport (if you are a non-resident director); 6. Address proof *i.e. Aadhaar card or voting card or bank statement or electricity bill (not older than 2 months) (if you are a resident director) or latest Bank statement or electricity bill or any other utility bill (not older than 2 months) (if you are a non-resident director); 7. Passport size photo; 8. Email ID (this will be your registered email id); and 9. Phone number (this will be your registered phone number).

I don’t remember my registered email id and/or phone number?

Once you provide us the details, LexStart will verify the details of the director to check if it matches with information provided on Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) website. In case there is a mismatch, then LexStart will help update the information of the director on the website of the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA).

Who has to file the form DIR-3 KYC?

Every individual with a DIN is required to file the form DIR-3 KYC, using his own Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) with MCA. The Form DIR-3 should be duly certified by a practising professional (CA/CS/CMA). LexStart will help you with the filing process.

What if I do not have a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) ?

A DSC is mandatory for filing this form DIR-3 KYC, which means that all directors will be required to obtain a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC). LexStart can help you with obtaining the DSC.
